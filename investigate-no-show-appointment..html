Introduction¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment.

Given features are:¶

  • Patient ID
  • Appointment ID
  • Gender: Patient gender, Categorical (Nomial)
  • Age: Patient age, Quantative
  • Appointment Day: Day of visit, Quantative
  • Scheduled Day: Day of call to register, Quantative
  • Neighbourhood: Hospital Location Categorical (Nominal)
  • Scholarship: whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program, Categorical
  • 4 features(Hipertension, Diabetes, Alcoholism, Handcape): true/false , Categorical
  • sms_received: whether or not confirmation message is sent to the patient, Categorical
  • no-show up: show patient commitment, Categorical (Yes --> uncommitted), (No --> committed)

After Analysis, we want to answer some Questions such as:¶

  1. Under different conditions, What are the proportion of uncommitted patients ?
  2. For how long Sms can affect patient commitment ?
  3. What features are helpful to predict patient commitment ?

Q2: For how long sms affect patient committement ?¶

p1 = proportion of committed patient in case of sms is received = num of committed sms_received patient / total num of sms_received patients

p2 = proportion of committed patient in case of sms is not received = num of committed not sms_received patient / total num of not sms_received patients

effect = p2 / p1

Conclusions¶

Results: Form dataset of patient no show appointment we can conclude that:

  • Under different conditions, proportion of patients don't show up to their commitment is about 26%
  • Most of patient aren't enrolled in Brasilian welfare program
  • Hospitals can save money and don't send sms to patients
  • Older patients are more committed to their appoinments
  • Fewer days between day of registeriation call and appointment day has a good effect on patient commitment

Limitations:

  • Most of variables are categorical, so we cannot show strong correlation between factors
  • Statistics used in our analysis are descriptive not inferential which means that we did not create inferences with our data
  • factors details are too few, which does not allow to draw conclusions
